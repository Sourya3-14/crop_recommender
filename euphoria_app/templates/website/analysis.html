📊 Dataset Overview

The Crop Recommendation dataset consists of 2,200 observations covering 22 different crop types. Each record contains seven environmental and soil-related features that influence crop growth: Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), Potassium (K), Temperature, Humidity, Soil pH, and Rainfall. The dataset is well balanced, with approximately 100 samples for each crop class, reducing the risk of class imbalance and improving model reliability.

📈 Feature Distributions

The histograms data illustrates the distribution of each numerical feature used by the machine learning model. They provide insight into the range and frequency of soil nutrients and climatic conditions present in the dataset. Understanding these distributions helps identify variations in environmental conditions and ensures that the model is trained on diverse agricultural scenarios.

🌾 Crop Distribution

This chart displays the number of samples available for each crop category. The nearly uniform distribution confirms that the dataset is balanced across all 22 crop classes, enabling the model to learn each crop equally without bias toward any particular class.

🔥 Feature Correlation Heatmap

The correlation heatmap visualizes the relationships among the input features. Most variables exhibit low to moderate correlations, indicating that each feature contributes unique information to the prediction process. This diversity of information improves the effectiveness of the machine learning model by minimizing redundancy between features.

Selected Model: Naive Bayes

Naive Bayes was selected as the final deployment model due to its superior performance, achieving an accuracy of 99.50%. In addition to its high predictive accuracy, the model offers fast inference, low computational requirements, and efficient memory usage, making it well suited for real-time web-based crop recommendation.
